Phase 4 · Returning Native Circulation

Weaning from CPB

Flow and drainage are reduced as deliberately as they were raised — every pre-wean condition must be confirmed first.

1

Closed loop communication
"Weaning from bypass"

2

Pre-wean checklist

Confirm every item before touching the venous line.

Pre-wean checklist

  • Core temperature 36 - 37°C
  • K⁺ / Ca²⁺ / pH / ABG corrected
  • Hb / Hct acceptable
  • Stable rhythm on ECG
  • Heart de-aired
  • Ventilation resumed
  • All monitors & lines checked

3

Communication

  • Partially occlude venous line — reduce drainage gradually
  • Allow the heart to fill
"Reducing venous drainage"

4

Reduce arterial flow in step

  • Match flow reduction to venous reduction
  • Watch for return of pulsatile arterial waveform and rising CVP

Key Values

Parameter Target
Reduction style Flow & drainage together
Target trace Pulsatile, CVP rising
Reversal agent Protamine
Confirm before leaving ACT normalised
Never reduce flow alone
Flow and venous drainage reduce together in small steps — exact mirror of going on bypass.

5

Communication

Assess native contractility on TOE.
Adjust inotropes / vasopressors as directed.
"Assessing cardiac function"

6

Off bypass

Once native cardiac output and BP are adequate, clamp the venous line.
"Off bypass"

7

Decannulate & reverse heparin

  • Venous then arterial cannula removed
  • Protamine given — confirm ACT normalised
"Bypass complete, protamine given"
