Phase 4 · Returning Native Circulation

Weaning from CPB

Flow and drainage are reduced as deliberately as they were raised — every pre-wean condition must be confirmed first.

1

Physiologic readiness for CPB separation

Patient parameters:

  • Temperature — ∼36–37°C (surgeon & procedure dependent)
  • ECG — sinus rhythm, +/- pacing
  • ABG — Hb/Hct, electrolytes (K⁺, Ca²⁺, Mg²⁺)
  • Volume

2

Ventilations on
"Are you happy with your ventilation?"

3

Surgeon: "Come down to 3/4 flow"

  1. First — fill the heart. Arterial waveform moves from flat to pulsatile; CVP rises 0 → 6.
  2. Second — decrease flow slowly to 3/4.
  3. Third — observe MAP.
  4. Communication

4

Continue to decrease flow

Continue to decrease flow under the surgeon's direction until target baseline CVP is reached.
Remember
Maintain closed loop communication throughout.

5

Surgeon: "Come off bypass"

Constant observation
Continuously observe the patient's hemodynamics post-CPB. Be ready for emergent re-initiation.
↓

Emergent Re-initiation
"Coming off bypass ..... venous is clamped .... arterial clamped .... off bypass"
  • Close manifold
  • Pause CO₂
  • Close purge line
  • Stop CPB timer
  • Stop reperfusion timer
  • Volatile off — Sevo, Iso
