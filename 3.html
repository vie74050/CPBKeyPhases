Aortic Cross-Clamp & Cardioplegia

The heart is electrically silenced and protected — every minute of ischaemic time is counted

1

Closed loop communication
"Going for cross-clamp"

2

Cross-clamp applied

Surgeon applies aortic cross-clamp, isolating the heart from systemic flow. Ischaemic clock starts.
"Cross-clamp on"

3

Deliver cardioplegia

  • Antegrade via aortic root and/or retrograde via coronary sinus
  • Cold blood or crystalloid per unit protocol
  • Monitor root/sinus pressure and delivered volume

Cardioplegia Dosing

Parameter Target
Route Antegrade / Retrograde
Temperature Cold blood or crystalloid
Antegrade press. 100–150 mmHg
Retrograde press. 30–40 mmHg
Redose interval Per unit protocol

4

Confirm arrest

  • Electromechanical silence on ECG
  • No mechanical activity on direct vision / TOE
  • Myocardium cooled — topical ice/slush as needed

Arrest Confirmation

  • Flat ECG trace
  • No mechanical activity
  • Myocardium cooled / firm
  • Cross-clamp time documented

5

Maintain protection

Repeat cardioplegia at protocol intervals. Communicate and document each dose, volume, and running cross-clamp time.
Ischaemic time is the clock that matters
Every redose interval and the final total cross-clamp time must be communicated and documented.

6

Coming off cross-clamp

  • De-air the heart
  • Confirm rewarming underway
"Coming off cross-clamp"

7

Cross-clamp removed

Note total ischaemic time. Watch for return of cardiac activity; defibrillation may be required.
"Cross-clamp off"
