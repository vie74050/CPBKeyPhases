Phase 1 · Pre-Bypass Verification

Testing
Arterial Line

Confirming circuit integrity, pulsatility, and line patency before the team commits to full cardiopulmonary bypass.
1
Closed loop communication
Perfusionist announces intent and surgeon/anaesthetist confirm before any clamp is touched. Nothing proceeds without verbal read-back.
“Testing arterial line”
2
Centrifugal only → RAP mode on
Isolate the centrifugal pump and enable Retrograde Autopriming / RAP mode in preparation for controlled volume testing.
3
Pulsatility check
  • ARemove arterial clamp
  • BTest volume — give 100 mL/s fast, observe reservoir volume; pressure should track up/down to baseline MAP
  • CRe-clamp arterial line — apply roller + centrifugal to Position 1, enable autoclamp, remove Clamp 3
    • ⚠Warning — centrifugal pumps carry retrograde flow risk during this test. Confirm pump head orientation and clamp sequence before removing the arterial clamp.
    4
    Communication
    Report findings back to the table.
    “Line tests well/good”
    5
    Centrifugal only → RAP mode off
    Stand down RAP mode. Circuit returns to standby configuration, ready for the initiation sequence.

    Circuit Reference

    Centrifugal pump → autoclamp 2 → clamp 1 → patient. Clamp 3 isolates the outlet during recirculation.

    Clamp Sequence

    • Add Clamp 3 (outlet, centrifugal pump)
    • Remove Clamp 1
    • Remove / open Autoclamp #2
    Before removing arterial clamp

    Confirm centrifugal pump head position and confirm with the team — retrograde flow through an unclamped arterial line is the single highest-risk moment of this test.
