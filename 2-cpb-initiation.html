Phase 2 · Going on Bypass

CPB
Initiation

The controlled handover from native circulation to the pump — flow and drainage are increased in lockstep, never alone.
1
Closed loop communication
Team confirms the move from native circulation to mechanical support before any clamp is released.
“Going on bypass”
2
Start timer
Bypass clock starts — every subsequent interval (cardioplegia, cross-clamp, total pump run) is measured from this point.
3
Communication
  • Open arterial clamp
  • Flow less than 500 mL/min
  • Venous open
    • “Arterial line on”
    4
    Communication
  • Open venous clamp slowly
  • Observe reservoir volume increasing
    • 5
    Increase flow / RPMs + increase venous drainage
    e.g. drain from patient — raise flow and drainage together, watching the four numbers on the monitor panel at every increment.
    6
    Confirm at full flow
  • Good drainage
  • Zero CVP
  • No ejection
  • Good line pressure
  • Colour difference in lines
    • 7
    Communication
    “Full flow”
